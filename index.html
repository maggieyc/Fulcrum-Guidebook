Introduction to the Fulcrum

The Fulcrum is the University of Ottawa’s independent English-language student news outlet. Founded in 1942, we’ve been keeping students (like you) informed about current events that directly affect the U of O community for 80 years. (Click here to read more!)


Our Mission: By holding those in positions of power accountable, the Fulcrum promotes the wellbeing of the University of Ottawa community through accessible and independent information from on and off-campus.


Our Vision: A University of Ottawa where every community member has the Fulcrum in the palm of their hand.

Land Acknowledgement

The Fulcrum acknowledges and respects that it stands on unceded Algonquin territory.

Volunteer With Us

The Fulcrum welcomes volunteers for both writing and multimedia in every section. Twice a year, we give new volunteers (contributors) the opportunity to become paid freelancers. Learn more on our website or by sending us an email at editor@thefulcrum.ca

First Nations activist Claudette Commanda to be the University of Ottawa’s first ever Indigenous chancellor

COMMANDA WILL STEP INTO THE ROLE ON NOV. 9

Claudette Commanda, an Algonquin Anishinabe from Kitigan Zibi Anishinabeg First Nation in Quebec, has been appointed chancellor of the University of Ottawa. She will succeed Calin Rovinescu following the end of his second mandate later this fall. Commanda is the first Indigenous person to be appointed in the university’s history.

Pedro's Tips and Tricks

  1. Students get 10% off at Loblaws on Tuesdays and Thursdays. You‘ll find a lot of places around Ottawa offer student discounts — just remember there‘s no harm in asking.
  2. Join a club or team. Between over 360 clubs and 32 sports teams, the U of O has something for everyone.
  3. Memorize the tunnel system — you‘ll want it for the winter.
  4. Join Facebook groups like uOttawa Textbook exchange & Buy Nothing Sandy Hill to save some some extra mo-ney — you never know what might pop up.
